How is risk score calculated ?
The risk score for a bank's questionnaire submission by vendors involves several steps and factors as described below. Firstly, the system calculates an inherent_risk_rating (on a scale of 1-5) based on each vendor response to predefined questions that relate specifically to their category and associated risks: The calculation can be outlined in this formula for individual responses: \[ \text{Individual Risk Score} = \text{Inherent Risk Rating} \times \text{Risk Multiplier (if applicable)} \] Since vendors from different categories have varying levels of risk, the system applies a category-specific multiplier to adjust their base inherent risks. For example: \[ \text{Adjusted Individual Risk Score} = (\text{Inherent Risk Rating} \times 1.5) \] for vendors in high-sensitivity categories like Financial, assuming a risk_multiplier of 1.5 for such category as per bank's configuration: Then to find the total submission score from multiple answers or questions by one vendor and get an average inherent rating across all answered items: \[ \text{Total Submission Risk Score} = \sum (\text{Individual Adjusted Risks}) \] And finally, taking into account that vendors may not answer every question provided in the form: \[ \text{Baseline Inherent Rating (stored)} = \frac{\text{Total Submission Risk Score}}{\text{Number of Answered Questions}} \] This score represents an average risk based on all answers given. It is then used by bank users for final evaluations and recorded in the `questionnaire_submissions` table along with other details like justification text, follow-up actions needed, etc. The detailed process ensures that each vendor's submission receives a fair but individualized risk assessment tailored to their answers and category sensitivity level as per bank policies.
